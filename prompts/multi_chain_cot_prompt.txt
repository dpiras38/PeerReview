grounded in evidence from the paper when possible.

Criteria:
1. Originality - Does the paper tackle a novel problem or introduce a novel approach?

2. Motivation - Is the problem clearly motivated, significant, and well-grounded in prior literature?

3. Technical Quality - Are the methods sound, rigorous, and appropriately applied?
   Are there any theoretical or methodological limitations?
4. Empirical Validation - Are the results correct, rigorous, and supportive of the claims?
   Are the experiments comprehensive, and do they include fair baselines?
5. Clarity - Is the paper clearly written, well-structured, and accessible to the target audience?
   Are the figures, tables, and explanations helpful?

- A final overall impression (1-2 sentences) that synthesizes the strengths and weaknesses.

The output should be 2-3 paragraphs, written in a professional and neutral tone,
as if for an actual conference review.
